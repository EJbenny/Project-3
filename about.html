About Our Club

Manchester City Football Club is one of the most prominent football clubs in England and globally. It's based in Manchester, as the name suggests, and competes in the Premier League, the top tier of English football. Here's a breakdown of the club:

  • History:


    Manchester City was founded in 1880 as St. Mark's (West Gorton) and became Manchester City in 1894. The club has had its ups and downs over the years but has experienced a significant resurgence in recent decades.

  • Achievements:


    Manchester City has won numerous domestic and international titles. In England, they have won the Premier League multiple times, including in the recent past under the ownership of the Abu Dhabi United Group. They have also won the FA Cup, League Cup, and the now-defunct UEFA Cup Winners' Cup.

  • Home Ground:


    The club plays its home games at the Etihad Stadium, located in East Manchester. The stadium has a capacity of over 55,000 spectators and is one of the most modern stadiums in the country.

  • Rivalries:


    Manchester City has intense rivalries with Manchester United, their cross-town rivals, with whom they contest the Manchester derby. Matches between the two clubs are highly anticipated and fiercely contested.
